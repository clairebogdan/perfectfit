Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI)


There are 16 Myers-Briggs personality types.
Each type consists of 4 distinct type indicators (MBTIs)

Extroverted

Extroverts are energized when interacting with other people. They enjoy social environments and are good at multitasking.

iNtuition

Intuitive people focus on the big-picture, easily seeing patterns around them. They value innovation and creativity.

Thinking

Thinkers make decisions by using logical analysis. They value honesty and consistency. They may not consider feelings.

Judging

Judgers are people that tend to be organized and prepared. They like making plans and executing their plan.

Introverted

Introverts prefer to work alone or in small groups. They value alone time and prefer to work on one task at a time.

Sensing

Sensors are realistic people who focus on facts and details. They value common sense and past experiences.

Feeling

Feelers are sensitive people that value cooperation. They make decisions based on their personal feelings and the feelings of others.

Perceiving

Perceivers prefer to keep options open and do not feel the need to live life according to plan. They are able to act spontaneously and enjoy flexibility.
